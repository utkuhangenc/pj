Introduction

After exploring some of the series on the website I decided to make an analysis on Residential Property Prices Indices in Turkey. The reason behind this to analyze the housing bubble in Turkey and how does it differ by location.

RPPIs help people to further understand where to invest while buying properties. The guidelines on how these indices are explained in Handbook on Residential Property Prices Indices (RPPIs) prepared by Eurostat which can be accesesd through here.

First, I am going to analyze both the house price index and the hedonic house price index for Turkey and it’s three biggest cities: Istanbul, Izmir, Ankara. The reason I am doing this first is to show the difference between hedonic house price index and house price index. The need for the hedonic price index is explained by a study made by Türkiye Cumhuriyeti Merkez Bankasi/ Central Bank of Republic of Turkey in 2016. It states that: > This increase is widespread across the country where prices are even doubled in some regions. Our study performs a hedonic price adjustment for the housing market in Turkey, where we control for the price effects of increases in observed house characteristics in time.

Hedonic price index is a regression model based pricing properties’ chracteristics. This chracteristics include the built year of he property, number of toilets, the quality of the building and etc. Further information about these chracteristics and the methodology could be find here.

Preparing Data

By using get_series function, we can get the data we want from the EVDS website. As seen below, the function takes 3 arguments: Series names, start date and end date. All dates should be in dd-mm-YYYY format. There is another argument date_format but default is dmY.

As I stated before I will use the data for Turkey and it’s three biggest cities in my first analysis.

I will list the variable names that I used in this analysis and what they correspond on EVDS website in a list format.

  • TP.01TKFE = House price index (general)
  • TP.02TR10 = House price index (Istanbul)
  • TP.03TR51 = House price index (Ankara)
  • TP.04TR31 = House price index (Izmir)
  • TP.HKFE01 = Hedonic house price index (general)
  • TP.HKFE02 = Hedonic house price index (Istanbul)
  • TP.HKFE03 = Hedonic house price index (Ankara)
  • TP.HKFE04 = Hedonic house price index (Izmir)
#Getting house price index for Istanbul, Izmir and Ankara 

house_index <- get_series(series = c("TP.01TKFE","TP.02TR10", "TP.03TR51","TP.04TR31"), start_date = "01-01-2010", end_date = "01-04-2018" )
house_index <- house_index$items %>%
  select(-UNIXTIME) %>%
  transmute(date = Tarih, House_Istanbul = TP_02TR10, House_Ankara = TP_03TR51, House_Izmir = TP_04TR31, House = TP_01TKFE)

#Getting hedonic house price index for Istanbul, Izmi and Ankara

hedonic_index <- get_series(series = c("TP.HKFE02", "TP.HKFE03", "TP.HKFE04","TP.HKFE01"), start_date = "01-01-2010", end_date = "01-04-2018" )
hedonic_index <- hedonic_index$items %>% 
  select(-UNIXTIME) %>%
   transmute(date = Tarih, Hedonic_Istanbul = TP_HKFE02, Hedonic_Ankara = TP_HKFE03, Hedonic_Izmir = TP_HKFE04, Hedonic_House = TP_HKFE01)

df <- left_join(house_index, hedonic_index, by = "date") 

df1 <- df %>%
  gather(key=serie,value = value,-date) %>% 
  mutate(index_type = ifelse(serie %in% c("Hedonic_House", "Hedonic_Istanbul", "Hedonic_Izmir", "Hedonic_Ankara"), "Hedonic House Price Index", "House Price Index"))

These two graphs show hedonic house price index increases more gradually than house price index and this ensures better result comparison between years. Also, as seen from the graph Istanbul is finally settling down however Izmir is on the rise in terms of price indices. Ankara has below average as expected. Especially after 2015, house price indices increased precipitiously in general. This can be explained by the Turkish goverment’s increased focus on construction industry. It will be wise to remember that The CBRT introduced hedonic house price indices in 2010 after a big leap in construction output to control this growth.

However this data has some spelling errors which might cause some problems when graphing. That’s why I am going to use computer_friendlying function that you can see below to fix these errors.

#Sys.setlocale(locale="Turkish_Turkey.1254") #Windows 

#new text conversions are below

computer_friendlying<-function(mytext,spaceas="-"){
  mytext<-gsub(" ",spaceas,mytext)
  mytext<-gsub("ç","c",mytext)
  mytext<-gsub("s","s",mytext)
  mytext<-gsub("ğ","g",mytext)
  mytext<-gsub("ü","u",mytext)
  mytext<-gsub("ö","o",mytext)
  mytext<-gsub("ı","i",mytext)
  mytext<-gsub("Ç","C",mytext)
  mytext<-gsub("S","S",mytext)
  mytext<-gsub("G","G",mytext)
  mytext<-gsub("Ü","U",mytext)
  mytext<-gsub("Ö","O",mytext)
  mytext<-gsub("I","I",mytext)
  return(mytext)
}
